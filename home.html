Detecting Smurfing:
Competitive Integrity Through Behavioral Analytics

This project explores whether in game behavioral data can identify smurfing in Rocket League. By analyzing player satistics across rank tiers in Rocket League, we build behavioral profiles that reveal skill level regardless of account rank. Our goal is to detect experienced players on new accounts before traditional ranking systems can catch up.

What

What is Smurfing?

Smurfing is the act of a highly skilled player bypassing a game's skill-based matchmaking system by using a secondary, lower ranked account. This creates a deliverate skill imbalance, allowing the veteran player to dominate a less experienced opponent who believe they are playing against peers of their current rank.

Why It's Dangerous

The act of smurfing undermines competitive integrity by creating deliberate skill imbalance that corrupts matchmaking data and discourages new player retention.

Introduction

1. Intro

Smurfing is a phenomenon in online gaming where highly skilled players choose to create low-ranked accounts that deceive the competitive ranking system to play against lower skilled players. The reasoning for why players choose to do this is not clear to game developers, rule-abiding players, or even researchers. In 2024, an Ohio State University study surveyed 328 gaming-related, online users and found that 69% admitted to smurfing themselves. This phenomenon poses a persistent challenge for game developers in how to maintain a balanced competitive environment when deception starts at account creation. The consequences of smurfing ultimately undermine the competitive integrity of online games by altering the fairness and balance of competitive matchmaking. In doing so, it distorts the ranked progression system and creates a more toxic competitive environment. When left rampant, rule-abiding players begin to feel the emotional distress of playing at a disadvantage, causing a decline in player return rate.

Traditional detection and mitigation strategies primarily rely on outcome-based signals such as win–loss patterns, rating irregularities, or long-term account performance. These approaches often struggle to detect irregular behavior during the early phases of an account’s lifetime, prolonging the ability of smurfs to negatively impact matchmaking quality. This study aims to combat smurfing as early as possible by focusing on behavioral patterns within games. Replay data was collected through the Ballchasing.com API and processed to extract behavioral features across various competitive ranks of the game Rocket League. Unsupervised learning techniques are first employed to construct behavioral profiles that characterize gameplay patterns within competitive tiers. Players whose early gameplay exhibits significant deviation from these rank-specific behavioral distributions are assigned an anomaly score based on behavioral abnormality. To complement this unsupervised profiling, a robust XGB classification model is trained and optimized on the data, utilizing the unsupervised insights as additional predictors. This system’s combination of unsupervised insight and predictive analysis hope to give evidence that supports the usage of behavioral statistics in producing high performance anomaly analysis.

This research addresses the need for a more efficient and effective smurf detection system, by addressing detection from a behaviorally-focused understanding of smurfing. The findings of this study aim to inform future matchmaking research and allow for broader discussions about fairness and integrity in online competitive environments. By emphasizing behavioral evidence and early-stage inference, this work contributes a transparent and extensible methodology for smurf detection. The methodology used in this study hopes to complement existing ranking systems and offer insight applicable to broader competitive gaming contexts.

Comparing Statistics

In competitive online games like Rocket League, players are ranked by skill and matched against opponents of similar ability. The data here compares how players at three skill levels actually behave during a match — not whether they win or lose, but how they play. "Beginner" represents Bronze through Silver, "Intermediate" covers Gold through Platinum, and "Expert" includes Diamond through Supersonic Legend. Each stat measures an in-game action that improves with experience, creating a behavioral fingerprint of skill. When a new account shows expert-level behavior in these areas, it suggests the player may not actually be a beginner. Select a stat below to explore the differences.
